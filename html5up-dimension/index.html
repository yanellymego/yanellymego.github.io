Projects

Grand Challenges Initiative

Chapman Univerisity offers a Grand Challenges Initiative (GCI) program to STEM and Engineering students. This program takes place over the span of 2 years (4 semesters), ending with a symposium. My partner and I worked on tackling the technology illiteracy in the elderly.

Each semester was dedicated to a specific part of the project, such as performing research, collecting data, creating a prototype, and producing a final product. Based on our discoveries, we decided on creating an app for the elderly could help them improve their techology literacy. We narrowed down the topics that the elderly population we surveyed struggled with, and created simple tutorials to explain those features.


Mastery Project: The Waiting Game

Relevant Experience

investiGATErs, TreeHacks 2023

This hackathon is hosted by Stanford University. My team and I created a project that would fulfill the education track. Though our main demographic is small children, anyone with an interest in learning about logic gates can benefit from our game. The user follows Allie the Alligator, or the gator of their choosing, as she makes her way through her home swamp. As Allie swims, she encounters LOGic gates that prompt the user to answer questions in order to pass through them. The overall goal is for the user to unlock the final LOGic gate with all the knowledge they have learned.

We built InvestiGATErs using Unity. We utilized multiple Youtube tutorials to help set up the game and game controls. We also used FIGMA to plan out the game. The biggest challenge we ran into was being overwhelmed by all the technology introduced at the plethora of workshops. Though we had a broad idea of what we wanted, we were stuck trying to incorporate too many elements into our project. Nevertheless, we were able to finalize our ideas and create something that we are passionate about.
